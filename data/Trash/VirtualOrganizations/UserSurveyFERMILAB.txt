---+User Satisfaction Survey - FERMILAB (alongwith KTEV, MINOS, MIPP)
For the record, FERMILAB VirtualOrganizations/VOInfo runs as the base VirtualOrganizations/VOInfo plus 12 subgroups
ACCELERATOR, ASTRO, CDMS, HYPERCP, KTEV, MINIBOONE, MINOS,
MIPP, NUMI, PATRIOT, TEST, THEORY, and NOVA.  Of those only MINIBOONE and MINOS are explicitly declared anywhere outside of Fermilab.  ASTRO, THEORY, NUMI, and NOVA are dormant at the moment.

---+++ Survey Answers

|*Q #*|*Q2 '07 Answers*|
| 1 | The Fermilab VirtualOrganizations/VOInfo uses OSG directly and as 12 different subgroups, each of which appear as the subgroup name in Gratia accounting  2 of these subgroups <br />(MINOS and MINIBOONE) are<br />declared to all OSG sites, the<br />rest of them are only declared at Fermilab at the moment.<br />They are ASTRO, ACCELERATOR, CDMS, HYPERCP, KTEV, MINIBOONE, MINOS, MIPP, NUMI, PATRIOT, TEST, THEORY, and NOVA.<br />I would expect that Gratia should show entries for <br />FERMILAB, CDMS, KTEV, MINIBOONE, and MIPP because those are the groups that have<br />been doing grid-based jobs. The others were not using the OSG interfaces to our cluster during this time period.<br />The plots you show for base<br />FERMILAB, KTEV, and MIPP<br />are correct.  Most of the FERMILAB base group usage is our monitoring tool which polls osg sites several times daily to see which sites accept the Fermilab VO. |
| 2 | We expect to see a large increase in activity for CDMS, HYPERCP, MINIBOONE, MINOS, and PATRIOT over Q3. <br />MINIBOONE is beginning a huge monte carlo production and using all available cycles at Fermilab and branching out to elsewhere.  HYPERCP and MINOS have almost completed the transition to pure grid-based processing.<br /> We expect MIPP and KTEV to hold constant at current levels of usage. |
| 3 | We are mostly satisfied with <br />the OSG operations experience.<br />The problem of debugging sites which claim they support our VO, and don't, persists and there are still some GOC tickets that have been open since the fall of '06 on this topic. |

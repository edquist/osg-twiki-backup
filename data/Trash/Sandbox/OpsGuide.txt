---++Introduction

The [[http://www.iu.edu][Indiana University]] based [[http://igoc.ivdgl.indiana.edu/index.php][Grid Operation Center (iGOC)]] provides operations services for participants in the [[http://www.ivdgl.org][international Virtual Data Grid Laboratory (iVDGL)]] Grid3 project and the [[http://www.opensciencegrid.org][Open Science Grid (OSG)]]. Services include monitoring, problem notification, tracking, and maintaining services related to critical production hardware and services. 

The iGOC serves as a central point of contact for all Grid3 and OSG-ITB sites. iGOC Grid Technologists are availale during business hours (8-5 EST Mon-Fri) to answer questions and for help with troubleshooting user, application developer and site administrator problems. Operators are on duty 24x7 to open problems tickets and track ongoing Grid3 and OSG-ITB related issues. 


---++Problem Reporting and Discovery

Problems are typically discovered by iGOC technologists via observation of monitoring systems. Additionally, problems may be reported to the iGOC from end-users or others via the [[http://igoc.ivdgl.indiana.edu/test/index.php][trouble ticket submission webform]], e-mail (igoc@ivdgl.org), or phone (317-278-9699).

<h2>Problem Response</h2>
<p>When a problem is detected by the iGOC staff the following procedures will be executed immediately:</p>

<table border="0", cellpadding="5" width="600">
<tr><td valign="top">1.</td><td>Assess the problem priority, according to the criticality of the service or
components.</td></tr> 
<tr><td valign="top">2.</td><td>Create a trouble ticket in the iGOC Footprints Project</td></tr>
<tr><td valign="top">3.</td><td>Perform problem notification, guided according to criticality, as:</td></tr>
<tr><td valign="top">&nbsp;</td><td>
<table border="1" cellpadding="10" width="600">
<tr>
<td>Critical
<td>The iGOC contacts the on-call person by phone immediately. If the on-call person doesn't respond, the iGOC contacts the
next person in the site contact rotation or backup.
</tr>
<tr><td>High
<td>The iGOC sends e-mail to the site contact immediately and a follow-up phone call is made by the iGOC to the site contact.
</tr>
<tr><td>Elevated
<td>The iGOC sends e-mail to the site contact immediately. Follow up will be made within 24 hours.
</tr>
<tr><td>Normal
<td>The iGOC sends e-mail to the site contact during business hours. No phone contact is necessary and follow up will be conducted as time allows.
</tr>
</table>
</td></tr>
<tr><td valign="top">4.</td><td>Perform standard problem tracking and follow-up.</td></tr>
</table>

---++Problem Tracking

<p>All problems worked by the iGOC will be tracked in the iGOC Footprints Ticketing system utilizing the priority scheme difined under *Problem Response*, in addition to whatever tracking methods the sites employ.
The iGOC tickets will record all e-mail, phone contact, instant message, and actions taken. Because the iGOC and sites utilize independent tracking systems the
ticket description should record respective ticket numbers. When a ticket is closed a phone call or e-mail should be exchanged between the site and iGOC.</p>

<p>The iGOC will on occasion have to work directly with the <a href="http://www.cs.wisc.edu/vdt/support.html">VDT Support Team</a>. In these cases the iGOC will
open a ticket in Footprints and open a ticket with VDT
Support by mailing <a href="mailto:vdt-support@ivdgl.org">vdt-support@ivdgl.org</a>. This will automatically open a VDT ticket. The iGOC will track the VDT
ticket number and all responses from the VDT Support group in the Footprints ticket and relay pertinent information to the customer. The VDT Support Team
may also need to open iGOC tracking tickets, these will be handled in the same manner as any customer ticket. VDT ticket information can be found at the <a
href="http://www.cs.wisc.edu/vdt/support/tickets/index.html">VDT Support Tickets</a> web page.</p>

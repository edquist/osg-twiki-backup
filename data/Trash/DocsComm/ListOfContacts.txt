---++ US IT Media list

U.S. IT Media list that I (KY, from Aug 06) reserve for more important news 
(such as the news that the OSG is getting funded!). I have done some 
 updating since it was originally made, but you will likely get some errors 
 that people's addresses are no longer good (the last time I used this was 
 in February -- must be 06).

(KY Jan07) The US IT media list is supposed to contain grid-interested media from the US. It was originally formed right after I started, in May 2005, by me going through the yellow book in the Public Affairs office that lists pretty much all US (national) media. In many cases I just wrote down people that were listed under IT, but for all I know they write about gadgets and not infrastructure. Many of these people probably have no interest in grid, and the Word document is probably hopelessly out of date here. I'd start from the email list (many people in media change jobs often, and thus I'm always removing people from the list). What I wanted to do was add people whenever I saw an article on grid (especially OSG) by them. This assumes I can find an email address (the yellow book has them, but they're not always easy to find on the Web). But I never really put that into practice.

---++ Grid PR List (Important people in grid world)
 
The Grid PR list contains important people in the OSG's world that we want to know about OSG News. This includes people from the funding agencies, heads of grid and network projects around the world, and a few media people (from GRIDtoday, Supercomputing Online) that will carry pretty much any OSG news that we put out. It also includes communications officers from other grid projects. Basically, as I met or had contact with communications officers or bigwigs from projects who might be interested in OSG news I added them to this list. But I'm sure there are some that have been forgotten (or who might need to be removed).

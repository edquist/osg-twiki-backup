The Open Science Grid (OSG) advances science through open distributed computing. The OSG is a multi-disciplinary partnership to federate local, regional, community and national cyberinfrastructures to meet the needs of research and academic communities at all scales.

---++ OSG News - July 2012

<b>Excerpt from the Press Release at the University of Wisconsin following the announcement of the discovery of a "Higgs like particle at the LHC".. </b>

When scientists at the Large Hadron Collider in Europe announced the appearance of a new particle among the pieces of smashed protons, Miron Livny saw a huge scientific success. But the University of Wisconsin–Madison computer scientist reveled in more than a fascinating research finding. “It’s also a huge triumph for mankind,” says Livny, chief technology officer at the Wisconsin Institutes for Discovery. “There were more than 40 nations that came together for a long time to do this one thing that — even if it all worked out — wasn’t going to make anyone rich. It’s a powerful demonstration of the spirit of collaboration.”

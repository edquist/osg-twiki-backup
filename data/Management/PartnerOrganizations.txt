---++ Introduction
The Open Science Grid maintains a partner relationship with many other organizations that are related grid infrastructures, other high performance computing infrastructures, international consortia, and certain project organizations that operate in the broad space of high throughput or high performance computing.
This page references active OSG Partners with links to basic information on the partnership.

---++ OSG Partners 
Below is a list of active OSG Partners with links to the information on the partner and our joint expectations from the partnership.

---+++ CDIGS
[[http://confluence.globus.org/display/CDIGS/CDIGS+Home+Page][CDIGS]] is the Community Development and Improvement of Globus Software.
%BR%
%BR%
Reason for Relationship
%BR%
Point of contact

---+++ CILogon
[[http://www.cilogon.org/service][CILogon]] is a project that provides an open source, standards-based cyberinfrastructure (CI) logon service, providing the NSF research community with credentials for secure access to the U.S. CI that bridges the identity credentials generated by the nation’s universities to a credential that will satisfy the authentication and authorization needs of NSF’s CI projects.
%BR%
%BR%
Authentication and authorization are important components of the OSG and inter-working with the NSF science research community approaches is important to make OSG open to all science users.
%BR%
Point of contact

---+++ EGI
[[http://www.egi.eu/][EGI]] is the European Grid Initiative that coordinates federation of the National Grid Infrastructures (NGI) in Europe.  This is an follow-on to the EGEE (Enabling Grids for E-sciencE) project.
%BR%
%BR%
EGI is the European equivalent of OSG and inter-working with EGI is critical to OSG's high energy physics stakeholders.
%BR%
Point of contact

---+++ EMI
[[http://www.eu-emi.eu/][EMI]] is the European Middleware Initiative.
%BR%
%BR%
Reason for Relationship
%BR%
Point of contact

---+++ !FutureGrid
[[http://futuregrid.org/][FutureGrid]] is a project to study the computer science aspects of use and security of computing grids and clouds.
%BR%
%BR%
OSG is interested in the study of use and security of computing grids as a core element in our mission.
%BR%
Point of contact

---+++ !GridColumbia
[[http://www.renata.edu.co/index.php/proyectos-en-realizacion-convocatorias-2009-a-l/1309-grid-colombia-servicio-de-computacion-en-grilla-nacional-a-traves-de-renata.html][GridColombia]] is the Colombia, South America National Grid.
%BR%
%BR%
We are partnering with various universities in Columbia to assist them in establishing a national grid in Columbia which is expected to become a partner grid with the OSG. 
%BR%
[[Main.JoseCaballero][Jose Caballero]] is the OSG interface to !GridColumbia.

---+++ !GridUNESP
[[http://www.unesp.br/grid/][GridUNESP]] is Sau Paulo State University's multi-campus computational grid.
%BR%
%BR%
!GridUNESP is a South American grid that will be a partner grid with the OSG.
%BR%
[[Main.JoseCaballero][Jose Caballero]] is the OSG interface to !GridUNESP.

---+++ Megellan
[[http://www.nersc.gov/nusers/systems/magellan/][Megellan]] is the National Energy Research Scientific Computing Center (NERSC) testbed for examining the potential for cloud computing for scientific applications.
%BR%
%BR%
Several OSG sites provide cloud computing facilities and OSG is also interested in the potential for cloud computing for scientific applications.
%BR%
Point of contact

---+++ NCI
[[http://nci.org.au/][NCI]] is the National Computational Infrastructure to provide Australian researchers with high end computing resources.  This was formerly the Australian Partnership for Advanced Computing (APAC). 
%BR%
%BR%
Reason for Relationship
%BR%
Point of contact

---+++ NEES
[[http://nees.org/][NEES]] is the Network for Earthquake Engineering Simulation.
%BR%
%BR%
NEES is a large user of computing resources and is experimenting with moving some of their computation to the OSG.
%BR%
Point of contact

---+++ NGS
[[http://www.ngs.ac.uk/][NGS]] is National (UK) Grid Service for academic research.
%BR%
%BR%
Reason for Relationship
%BR%
Point of contact

---+++ OGF
[[http://www.gridforum.org/][OGF]] is the Open Grid Forum that shares best practices and consolidates these best practices into standards for distributed computing.
%BR%
%BR%
OSG is both interested in and a contributor to best practices for distributed computing.
%BR%
Point of contact

---+++ Pegasus
[[http://pegasus.isi.edu/index.php][Pegasus]] is a project that encompasses a set of technologies the help workflow-based applications execute in high throughput and high performance cyberinfrastructures.
%BR%
%BR%
As a primary workflow manager for OSG, Pegasus is an important partner and collaborator on projects.
%BR%
[[Main.EwaDeelman][Ewa Deelman]] leads the Pegasus project and is the OSG contact.

---+++ !TeraGrid
[[https://www.teragrid.org/][TeraGrid]] is an open scientific discovery infrastructure combining leadership class resources at eleven partner sites to create an integrated, persistent computational resource.
%BR%
%BR%
!TeraGrid is another component in the US Cyber-infrastructure that shares software and inter-works with OSG.
%BR%
There are many !TeraGrid contacts.  John Towns is a primary contact for OSG and [[Main.RuthPordes][Ruth Pordes]] is OSG's primary interface.

---+++ WLCG
[[http://lcg.web.cern.ch/lcg/][WLCG]] is the Worldwide Large Hadron Collider (LHC) Computing Grid.
%BR%
%BR%
The LHC project is a primary stakeholder of OSG and OSG is part of the Worldwide Computing Grid.
%BR%
Point of contact

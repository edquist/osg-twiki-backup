---++ Introduction
The Open Science Grid maintains a partner relationship with many other organizations that are related grid infrastructures, other high performance computing infrastructures, international consortia, and certain project organizations that operate in the broad space of high throughput or high performance computing.
This page references active OSG Partners with links to basic information on the partnership.

---++ OSG Partners 
Below is a list of active OSG Partners with the:
   1 definition of the Partner, including a link to the the Partner,
   1 reason we are partnering, and 
   1 OSG and Partner Contacts.

---+++ CDIGS
   1 [[http://confluence.globus.org/display/CDIGS/CDIGS+Home+Page][CDIGS]] is the Community Development and Improvement of Globus Software.
   1 OSG provides needs to CDIGS and wants to ensure that the development evolution of Globus continues to inter-work with OSG software. OSG works with CDIGS on the scalability and functional testing of new components needed by the OSG community.
   1 [[Main.ChanderSehgal][Chander Sehgal]] and [[Main.AlainRoy][Alain Roy]] are the OSG interfaces to Globus.  [[http://www.ci.uchicago.edu/people/profile.php?id=881][Steve Tuecke]] is the technical lead of Globus and is the contact with the OSG.

---+++ CILogon
   1 [[http://www.cilogon.org/service][CILogon]] is a project that provides an open source, standards-based cyberinfrastructure (CI) logon service, providing the NSF research community with credentials for secure access to the U.S. CI that bridges the identity credentials generated by the nation’s universities to a credential that will satisfy the authentication and authorization needs of NSF’s CI projects.
   1 Authentication and authorization are important components of the OSG. OSG plans to support authentication provided by the CILogon service and is working with the project to this end. 
   1 [[Main.MineAltunay][Mine Altunay]] is the OSG interface to CILogon. [[Main.JimBasney][Jim Basney]] is the PI of CILogon and is the contact with OSG.

---+++ EGI
   1 [[http://www.egi.eu/][EGI]] is the European Grid Initiative that coordinates federation of the National Grid Infrastructures (NGI) in Europe.  This is an follow-on to the EGEE (Enabling Grids for E-sciencE) project.
   1 EGI is the European equivalent of OSG and inter-working with EGI is critical to OSG's high energy physics stakeholders.
   1 [[Main.RuthPordes][Ruth Pordes]] and [[http://www-tele.fnal.gov/cgi-bin/telephone.script?type=name_last&accuracy=contains&string=LIVNY][Miron Livny]] are the OSG contacts. [[http://www.egi.eu/about/staff/][Steve Newhouse]] is the project director of EGI and is the contact with OSG.

---+++ EMI
   1 [[http://www.eu-emi.eu/][EMI]] is the European Middleware Initiative. The ARC, gLite, UNICORE and dCache middleware providers have joined forces to support and evolve the software used by the European and international research infrastructures. 
   1 The OSG Virtual Data Toolkit software stack must continue to  interoperate with the EMI software stack as the infrastructures evolve.
   1 [[Main.AlainRoy][Alain Roy]] is  the OSG contact and attends the weekly Engineering Middleware Taskforce meetings. [[http://consult.cern.ch/xwho/people/481794][Alberto DiMiglio]] is the Director of EMI and is the contact with the OSG.

---+++ ESnet
   1 [[http://www.es.net/][ESnet]] is is a high-speed network serving thousands of Department of Energy (DOE) scientists at over 40 institutions. 
   1 
   1 

---+++ !FutureGrid
   1 [[http://futuregrid.org/][FutureGrid]] is a project to study the computer science aspects of use and security of computing grids and clouds.
   1 OSG is interested in the study of use and security of computing grids as a core element in our mission.
   1 [[Main.RuthPordes][Ruth Pordes]] is on the !FutureGrid User Advisory Board and is  the OSG contact.  [[http://futuregrid.org/contact][Geoffrey Fox]] is the PI of !FutureGrid.

---+++ !GridColombia
   1 [[http://www.renata.edu.co/index.php/proyectos-en-realizacion-convocatorias-2009-a-l/1309-grid-colombia-servicio-de-computacion-en-grilla-nacional-a-traves-de-renata.html][GridColombia]] is the Colombian National Grid.  [[http://translate.google.com/translate?hl=en&sl=es&u=http://www.renata.edu.co/&prev=/search%3Fq%3Dhttp://www.renata.edu.co%26hl%3Den%26safe%3Dactive%26sa%3DG%26prmd%3Div][GridColombia site in English]]
   1 We are partnering with various universities in Colombia to assist them in establishing a national grid in Colombia which is expected to become a partner grid with the OSG. 
   1 [[Main.JoseCaballero][Jose Caballero]] is the OSG interface to !GridColombia.  [[http://puj-portal.javeriana.edu.co/portal/page/portal/facultad_ingenieria/espanol/sistemas/profesores/enrique_gonzalez][Enrique González]], director of !GridColombia, is OSG's primary contact.

---+++ !GridUNESP
   1 [[http://www.unesp.br/grid/][GridUNESP]] is São Paulo State University's statewide, multi-campus computational grid.  [[http://translate.google.com/translate?hl=en&sl=pt&tl=en&u=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.unesp.br%2Fgrid%2F][GridUNESP site in English]]
   1 !GridUNESP is a South American grid that will be a partner grid with the OSG. More information about !GridUNESP's configuration can be found on the [[https://www.ncc.unesp.br/cgi-bin/twiki/view][NCC TWiki]] as well as the [[VirtualOrganizations.DOSAR_GridUNESP_OSG][OSG VO Twiki]].
   1 [[Main.HorstSeverini][Horst Severini]] is the OSG liaison to !GridUNESP.  [[http://www-tele.fnal.gov/cgi-bin/telephone.script?type=name_last&accuracy=contains&string=NOVAES][Sergio Novaes]] is the director of !GridUNESP and OSG's primary contact.

---+++ Internet2
   1 [[http://www.internet2.edu/][Internet2]] is a U.S. advanced networking consortium of the research and education community of 200 U.S. universities, 70 corporations, and 45 government agencies and other institutions. The consortium provides leading-edge network development, deployment, and capabilities to its members.

   1 

---+++ Magellan
   1 [[http://www.nersc.gov/nusers/systems/magellan/][Magellan]] is    a joint project between Argonne National Laboratory (ANL) and the National Energy Research Scientific Computing Center (NERSC) to build and operate a testbed for examining the potential for cloud computing for scientific applications.
   1 Several OSG sites provide cloud computing facilities and OSG is also interested in the potential for cloud computing for scientific applications.
   1 [[Main.ChanderSehgal][Chander Sehgal]] and [[Main.IgorSfiligoi][Igor Sfiligoi]] are the OSG interfaces to Magellan.  [[http://www.alcf.anl.gov/about/dir_detail.php?id=236][Susan Coughlin]] and [[http://www.lbl.gov/cgi-bin/ds/ds.cgi?include=n&last_search=Coughlin&repeat=&peopleName=canon][Shane Canon]] are the Magellan contacts to OSG. 

---+++ NEES
   1 [[http://nees.org/][NEES]] is the Network for Earthquake Engineering Simulation.
   1 NEES is a large user of computing resources and is experimenting with moving some of their computation to the OSG.
   1 [[Main.RuthPordes][Ruth Pordes]] is the chair of the Computing Infrastructure Sub-Committee of the Project Advisory Committee of NEES and is the OSG contact. [[https://engineering.purdue.edu/ECE/People/profile?resource_id=3240][Rudi Eigenmann]] is the contact from NEES to OSG. 

---+++ NGS
   1 [[http://www.ngs.ac.uk/][NGS]] is National (UK) Grid Service for academic research.
   1 NGS supports the OSG Virtual Data Toolkit as one of the middleware stacks used on its resources.
   1 [[http://www-tele.fnal.gov/cgi-bin/telephone.script?type=name_last&accuracy=contains&string=LIVNY][Miron Livny]] is the OSG contact. David Wallom is the NGS contact to OSG.

---+++ NYSGrid
   1 [[http://www.google.com/][NYSGrid]] is a collaboration among New York institutions to create an advanced grid infrastructure that will strengthen research and education capabilities across the State.
   1 The NYSGrid is a partner grid with OSG.
   1 

---+++ OGF
   1 [[http://www.gridforum.org/][OGF]] is the Open Grid Forum that shares best practices and consolidates these best practices into standards for distributed computing.
   1 OSG is both interested in and a contributor to best practices for distributed computing.
   1 [[Main.RuthPordes][Ruth Pordes]] and [[http://www-tele.fnal.gov/cgi-bin/telephone.script?type=name_last&accuracy=contains&string=LIVNY][Miron Livny]] are the OSG contacts.  See also [[http://www.ogf.org/gf/group_info/areasgroups.php][OGF Active Groups]] for contacts.

---+++ Pegasus
   1 [[http://pegasus.isi.edu/index.php][Pegasus]] is a project that encompasses a set of technologies the help workflow-based applications execute in high throughput and high performance cyberinfrastructures.
   1 As a primary workflow manager for OSG, Pegasus is an important partner and collaborator on projects.
   1 [[Main.BurtHolzman][Burt Holzman]] is the OSG interface to Pegasus.  [[Main.EwaDeelman][Ewa Deelman]] leads the Pegasus project and is the OSG contact.

---+++ !TeraGrid
   1 [[https://www.teragrid.org/][TeraGrid]] is an open scientific discovery infrastructure combining leadership class resources at eleven partner sites to create an integrated, persistent computational resource.
   1 !TeraGrid is another component in the US Cyber-infrastructure that shares software and inter-works with OSG.
   1 [[Main.RuthPordes][Ruth Pordes]] is OSG's primary interface to !TeraGrid.  [[http://www.ncsa.illinois.edu/AboutUs/People/contact.php?id=jtowns][John Towns]] is a primary contact to OSG.

---+++ WLCG
   1 [[http://lcg.web.cern.ch/lcg/][WLCG]] is the Worldwide Large Hadron Collider (LHC) Computing Grid.
   1 The LHC project is a primary stakeholder of OSG and OSG is part of the Worldwide Computing Grid.
   1 [[Main.RuthPordes][Ruth Pordes]] and Michael Ernst are members of the WLCG management board and are the OSG contacts. [[https://twiki.cern.ch/twiki/bin/view/Main/IanBird][Ian Bird]] is the primary contact to OSG.

---++ Scope

The newsletter is to let people know specific things happening in OSG and to provide technical and practical information for readers, ie. what internet2 tools will be in VDT and when, what insight to using them is available and at what URL, etc...  Other parties are more than welcome to suggest idea's for upcoming articles.

Each technical brief should be no more than 100-120 words.

---++ Process

   * Jemise will contact Ashu the 1st of the month for the script for LHC metric data on the 15th of every month so Jemise so she can compile for newsletter.

   * Chander and Ruth will assist in suggestions for articles based on Area Coordinator or other meetings.

   * First step will be to include all suggested articles for the month into the OSG Commnications under newsletter in Google Site  (https://sites.google.com/site/osgcommunications/). 
 
   * Jemise will press people for paragraphs about their activities. Jemise will receive paragraphs and will upload into the Google Site.

   * Once uploaded Jemise will forward the articles to Miriam Boon who may do further editing (semantics and content; articles that are very long or out of scope will be sent back   to  the author.

   * Miriam will edit then send email to Jemise and Ruth - if extensive editing Miriam to send to author, Ruth will edit and give comments, Ruth will re-send to Miriam to make corrections.  

   * Once all articles are approved by Ruth, Jemise will input the newsletter into dreamweaver then send to Ruth and Miriam for approval.

   * Once approved by Ruth, Miriam  will send to the Executive Board for approval.

   * After changes made from the EB upload the OSG newsletter into the OSG Newsetter archive ( http://www.opensciencegrid.org/newsletter_archive).

   * Once uploaded on the web the newsletter will be sent out by Jemise to osg@openscience.org and a reminder for Ruth Pordes to approve to be sent out.

---++ Including images

Use Photoshop Elements, or a similar graphics editing program to make smaller.

1. If you want to link to a larger image, first save original image as a .gif, .jpg or .png on your computer

2. Resize image so that height or width (whichever is bigger) is no more than 250 pixels.

3. Save image as filename_sm.gif/jpg/png on your computer

4. Upload both images to web site: [[http://www.opensciencegrid.org/Login][Login]]

   * In black bar on left side, click "image manager"
   * In Image Manager, click "newsletter" subdirectory on the left.
   * Upload both files from your computers

5. In newsletter template, insert an image. Use the full URL: _http://www.opensciencegrid.org/images/newsletter/filename_sm.jpg/.gif/.png_ (make sure you use the small image)

6. If you want to link to the larger image, link the image you just inserted to the large image. URL: _http://www.opensciencegrid.org/images/newsletter/filename.jpg/.gif/.png_


---++ Emailing newsletter via Thunderbird

1. First, send a test message to yourself and Marcia (from your own account) before you send it to osg. 

2. Make sure all links and images are correct. (Click on images to make sure larger image appears, if necessary).

3. Once you're happy with the layout, compose new mail message.

4. Select osgnews@opensciencegrid as the sender. Mail to osg@opensciencegrid.

5. Click in the "body" part of the message and

   A. from the menu, select "Insert" > "HTML" (a box will pop up for you to insert the HTML code)

   B. open the Dreamweaver code and select all

   C. paste the code in the box.

6. Send message as HTML only

7. Make sure Ruth will be around to release the newsletter promptly. (Once released, the newsletter will be timestamped with the time it was sent originally. If there is a delay of a day, it will still show up as being sent the day before.)

---++Posting Newsletter on the OSG Web Site
1. Login into the Xenomedia content management system: http://www.opensciencegrid.org/Login

2. Create a Page
   * Menu Name: OSG Newsletter Month Year (eg. OSG Newsletter July 2009)
   * Page Title: same as above
   * Content: Plain HTML
   * "Online" box is checked.

3. Paste contents of newsletter into page
   * Reformat as necessary (usually just making sure article titles remain bold and that the newsletter date is at the top.)
   * Add a link at the bottom to "OSG Newsletter Archive" that is linked to http://www.opensciencegrid.org/?pid=1000354

4. Save file above, MAKE NOTE OF THE PID # for step #6 and copy all contents of the page.

5. Paste contents of above into the current newsletter page: https://www.opensciencegrid.org/?pid=1000351

6. Update the newsletter archive page: http://www.opensciencegrid.org/?pid=1000354

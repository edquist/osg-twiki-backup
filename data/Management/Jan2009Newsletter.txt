<h1><strong>OSG Newsletter, January, 2009</strong></h1><p>&nbsp;</p><p><strong>From the Executive Director</strong></p> <p>While the usage of OSG continued to grow and the number of new users continued to expand, your OSG project team was consumed by preparations for our DOE SciDAC and NSF Continuation review. This is now over and it looks like the reviewers &ldquo;smiled.&quot; Now we are back to needed activities for our stakeholders. We look forward to seeing all of you at the <a href="http://indico.fnal.gov/conferenceDisplay.py?confId=2012" target="_blank">All Hands Meeting in March</a> &ndash; please register soon. </p> <p>With best wishes for 2009 </p> <p>~ Ruth </p> <hr /> <p><strong>OSG SecurityTeam News</strong></p> <p>The OSG SecurityTeam Team is aware of ongoing attacks using stolen ssh passwords and keys. We recommend that OSG members:</p> <blockquote>   <p>* Protect ssh private keys with good passphrases. </p>   <p>* Use firewalls to restrict ssh access so that ssh connections are allowed only from trusted hosts. </p>   <p>* Promptly apply security updates from software vendors, including operating system kernel updates. <a href="https://twiki.grid.iu.edu/bin/view/SecurityTeam/BestPractices%20" target="_blank">See more detailed recommendations</a>.</p> </blockquote> <p>OSG members should always promptly report security incidents involving OSG resources according to <a href="https://twiki.grid.iu.edu/bin/view/SecurityTeam/IncidentDiscoveryReporting" target="_blank">these instructions</a>.</p> <p>The SecurityTeam Team has published a Privacy Policy and an update to the Certificate Authority Policy. Please review the <a href="https://twiki.grid.iu.edu/bin/view/SecurityTeam/PoliciesProcedures" target="_blank">security policies</a>. The SecurityTeam Team can be contacted at <a href="mailto:security@opensciencegrid.org">security@opensciencegrid.org</a>.</p> <p>~ Jim Basney </p> <hr /> <p><strong>OSG and ALICE working to enable AliEn VO-Box paradigm on OSG</strong></p> <p>The LHC experiment, <a href="http://aliceinfo.cern.ch/" target="_blank">ALICE</a>,  and OSG are working together to enable ALICE&#39;s specialized <a href="http://alien.cern.ch/twiki/bin/view/AliEn/Home" target="_blank">AliEn</a> framework on the OSG facility. AliEn is a lightweight grid framework built around open-source components using a combination of web services and a distributed agent model. It is developed by the ALICE collaboration as a production environment for the simulation, reconstruction, and analysis of physics data.</p> <p>Central to AliEn is the VO-Box model. Each site officially supporting the ALICE VO provisions a VO-Box--a dedicated cluster node on which long-lived agents and services are deployed--to support ALICE VO services. Responsibility of a VO-Box is shared directly between the ALICE VO and the site.</p> <p>OSG created the Joint <a href="https://twiki.grid.iu.edu/bin/view/Trash/Trash/Trash/Trash/VirtualOrganizations/ALICE_OSG" target="_blank">ALICE-OSG Task force</a> last November to enable the paradigm in the US on the OSG. Participants include ALICE members from the EU and the US, various OSG groups, and NERSC as the first site.</p> <p>The joint task force has successfully enabled AliEn job submissions from a VO-Box at NERSC using OSG 1.0. Preliminary evaluation of a hybrid VO-Box approach was also done, using remote submissions from this VO-Box to LBNL and GLOW sites on top of different batch systems.</p> <p>Near term plans include identifying a certificate renewal mechanism for ALICE OSG operations, to be followed by a stress-testing exercise at NERSC to evaluate scalability of AliEn with OSG software. Once the scalability is certified to be satisfactory, the plan is to expand the operations to other ALICE sites in the US.</p> <p>~ Abhishek Singh Rana </p> <hr /> <p><strong>Setting Realistic Expectations for Network Usage</strong></p> <p>One of the major obstacles to effectively using Grid infrastructure is the lack of a clear understanding about how the network resources will be used. Saying that an experiment requires 3.9 Gbps is much less informative that saying it requires moving 2-3 TBs of data in 3-5 hours every 10-14 days. The latter description can easily be turned into network utilization rates and also shows the cyclic nature of these flows. Simply stating a transfer rate can unwittingly set the wrong expectations for network operators.</p> <p>Moving a couple of Terabytes in a few hours is a reasonable network demand that any OSG site should be able to support. .Failure to achieve this rate should lead to an investigation to find, and possibly eliminate, the bottlenecks that are degrading your performance.</p> <p>~ Richard Carlson</p> <hr /> <p><strong>Internet2/OSG Partnership</strong></p> <p>Internet2 and OSG are expanding their collaboration to enhance the deployment and use of advanced network measurement &amp; monitoring tools. </p> <p>Internet2 staff, with its international partners, have developed a suite of tools and services that can be used to create a viable multi-domain measurement &amp; monitoring infrastructure. Internet2 will lead efforts to assist in the widespread deployment of these tools and services, and just as importantly, will lead efforts to train OSG administrator and users on the use of these tools and services.</p> <p>~ Richard Carlson  </p> <hr /> <p><strong>OSG virtual cluster available</strong></p> <p>The <a href="http://workspace.globus.org/" target="_blank">Nimbus toolkit</a>  team made available an <a href="http://workspace.globus.org/vm/osg-cluster-1.0.0.html" target="_blank">OSG virtual cluster</a>  that can be deployed on-demand on any platforms participating in the <a href="http://workspace.globus.org/clouds/" target="_blank">Science Clouds</a> test bed. The cluster is composed of Xen virtual machines that can be further customized by the user to install application-specific software or add user information. A similar cluster has been used by the STAR community for runs on the Science </p> <p>Clouds as well as Amazon EC2. The availability of an OSG virtual </p> <p>cluster provides an easy way for OSG users to experiment with cloud computing and virtualization technologies. In order to experiment, a user follows a 3-step process: (1) request access to one of the Science Clouds, (2) download a simple client, and (3) deploy the cluster by </p> <p>following a few simple instructions. The Science Clouds test bed </p> <p>comprises several EC2-style clouds available in the academic domain. The clouds are configured with the Nimbus software that provides an open source implementation of Amazon&#39;s EC2 interfaces. Wispy, one of the Science Clouds available at Purdue University, is a part of the TeraGrid. The ability to easily deploy a virtual OSG cluster on a TeraGrid resource is a promising implementation of an OSG-TeraGrid gateway.</p> <p>~ Kate Keahey, Argonne National Laboratory </p> <hr /> <p><strong>OSG gets Big Bang </strong></p> <p>Last month, we asked you for information about which episode of the television program, <em>The Big Bang Theory</em> mentioned the OSG. Thanks to Bob Cousins and David Saltzberg of UCLA, we have an answer! In the opening scene of &quot;The Jerusalem Duality,&quot; a precocious 14 year-old post doc candidate named Dennis is introduced to the lead characters, Sheldon and Leonard, by the department chair.</p> <p>CHAIR: I thought you boys could show Dennis around. Let him see why we&#39;re the best physics research facility in the country.</p> <p>DENNIS: I already know you&#39;re not. You don&#39;t have an Open Science Grid computer or a free electron laser. And the string theory research being done here is nothing but a dead end. <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0017T7YYI" target="_blank">See Video</a>.</p> <p>And Preston Smith of Purdue wrote that a new episode, &quot;The Friendship Algorithm,&quot; aired last night: &quot;The main character apparently tries to curry favor of the local OSG site admin, who allegedly only lets his friends use the system. Of course in the end we find out that this isn&#39;t the case, and that there&#39;s &quot;schedules&quot; that guide that. :)&quot;</p> <p>&nbsp; </p>  <p>&nbsp; </p>

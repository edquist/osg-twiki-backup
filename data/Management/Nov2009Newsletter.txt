<meta http-equiv="Content-Type" content="text/html; charset=utf-8" /><title>OSG Newsletter, November 2009</title>   <table border="0" width="800">   </table> <div class="writeboardtitle">   <blockquote><h1 align="left">OSG Newsletter, November 2009</h1><p><b>____________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________</b></p>   <p><b>LHC Startup: Beams Circulated!<br />     <br />     </b>For more information, please see the following:</p>   <p><a href="http://twitter.com/cern" target="_blank">CERN Twitter site </a><br />   <a href="http://blogs.uslhc.us/" target="_blank">US LHC Blog</a>  </p>   <p>____________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________</p>   <p><b>Update on Production Runs of ROSETTA on OSG from Kuhlman Lab </b></p> </blockquote></div>    <blockquote><p>In the <a href="http://sites.google.com/site/kuhlmanlabwebpage">Kuhlman Lab</a> at the   University of North Carolina, we have developed a protocol that uses <span class="caps">PDB</span> files that contain structures of proteins bound to certain   peptide substances in order to scan for the substrates that will bind to a   target epitope&mdash;the part of the molecule the immune system can attach to.   Specifically, we are investigating the protein structures that cause infectious   diseases, such as tuberculosis, malaria, and influenza.</p></blockquote>   <blockquote><p>We are using <span class="caps">ROSETTA</span>, a protein design program, to   attach additional residues to the peptide in the <span class="caps">PDB</span> file and to run design simulations to test the peptide&rsquo;s binding affinity.   From these results, the   best structure is selected based on the calculated binding energy and total   energy output by <span class="caps">ROSETTA</span>. We will then confirm our   results in the laboratory. Currently, two million starting decoys are being designed with <span class="caps">ROSETTA</span> on the <span class="caps">OSG</span>. Each decoy submits 50,000 jobs on the OSG per week, and currently, I have completed about one quarter of the decoys. </p></blockquote>   <blockquote><p> ~ Eun-Jung Choi</p><p>____________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________</p><div class="writeboardtitle">   <p><b>GHC Presentation</b></p> </div><p>The mission of the  <a href="http://anitaborg.org">Anita Borg Institute</a> for Women and Technology is to seek to increase the impact of women on all aspects   of technology and to increase the positive impact of technology on the world&rsquo;s   women. And this is exactly what it achieved again this fall when the institute   hosted their annual Grace Hopper Celebration (<span class="caps">GHC</span>) Of   Women in Computing.</p><p>I attended the conference representing both <span class="caps">LIGO</span> and   the <span class="caps">OSG</span> and presented &ldquo;On the Evolution of Large Scale   Distributed Systems <span class="caps">AND</span> High Throughput Computing on the   Open Science Grid Infrastructure.&rdquo; I shared the technical session with Corina   Stratan from Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam; Corina introduced the audience to the   main models of distributed systems and I gave a more detailed view of the   structure and function of the <span class="caps">OSG</span> and LIGO&rsquo;s role within   it. Our presentations were very well received and met with several   questions.</p><p>The world&rsquo;s largest gathering of women in computing, <span class="caps">GHC</span> increased by over 11 percent in size this year, with a   record 1608 attendees. The conference theme, &ldquo;Creating Technology for Social   Good,&rdquo; drew participants from industry, academia, and government. More than 173   companies and 210 universities were represented, with about half of attendees   being undergraduate and graduate students.</p><p>Over several parallel sessions top researchers presented their work, over one   hundred panels discussed wide-ranging topics, and numerous special sessions and   workshops focused on the role of women in today&rsquo;s technology fields.</p><p>The conference was hustling and bustling with activity from early morning   until evening. One could feel intensity and creativity in the air, as every   corner was occupied by groups of people talking, networking, and laughing.</p><p>Indeed, praise to these talented women who put together a very well organized   and content rich gathering-a success all around. I left feeling energized and   inspired.</p><p>~ Britta Daudert</p><p>____________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________</p><div class="writeboardtitle">   <p><b>OSG Staff Change News</b></p> </div><p>In December, Mats Rynge of the <span class="caps">OSG</span> Engagement team is   relocating with his family to Southern California to begin working on the   Pegasus team with Ewa Deelman at <span class="caps">USC</span>-<span class="caps">ISI</span>. For at least the near term, Mats will continue in his role   as the technical lead for the Engagement team from his new home   institution.  </p><p>____________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________</p><div class="writeboardtitle">   <p><b>Grid Colombia Workshop</b></p> </div></blockquote>                                             <blockquote><table border="0" width="997">     <tbody><tr>       <td width="730"><table align="left" border="0" cellpadding="3" cellspacing="3" width="250">   <tbody><tr>   <td align="center"><a href="http://www.opensciencegrid.org/images/newsletter/GridColombia.jpg"><img src="http://www.opensciencegrid.org/images/newsletter/GridColombia_250.jpg" border="0" height="188" width="250" /></a></td>   </tr>    </tbody></table>         <p>October&rsquo;s two-week <a href="http://indico.fnal.gov/conferenceDisplay.py?confId=2832">Grid Columbia Workshop</a> was an expanded   version of previous <span class="caps">OSG</span> grid schools. We partnered with   the PIs of the Grid Colombia initiative to meet their particular objectives as   part of a 3-phase program launching of a Colombian national grid initiative.   Jose Caballero (OSG&rsquo;s Latin America outreach coordinator), Rob Gardner, and Grid   Colombia&rsquo;s Harold Castro Barrera (Universidad de los Andes) and Enrique Gonzalez   Guerrero (Javeriana Universidad) led the workshop. Thirty-four participants took   part&mdash;university faculty, technical staff, and students from seventeen   universities throughout Colombia.</p>         <p>During the first week, we focused on &ldquo;Scientific Computing on Grids,&rdquo;   including fundamentals such as job submission, guest lectures and discussion,   and lab exercises illustrating use with production systems (Panda, GlideinWMS, <span class="caps">OSG</span>-MatchMaker and Swift) and new tools, such as the <span class="caps">OSG</span> storage discovery toolkit. Thanks to volunteers Burt   Holzman, Mike Wilde, John McGee and Mats Rynge, Brian Bockelman, Ted Hessleroth,   Pedro Salgado, and Marco Mambelli.</p>         <p>The second week involved &ldquo;Grid Infrastructure Building&rdquo; with the goal of   building a small test grid consisting of prototype Grid Colombia sites supported   by a &ldquo;site&rdquo; with central (<span class="caps">GOC</span>-like) services. Students   were divided into teams who built small clusters from scratch with an additional   &ldquo;GOC team&rdquo; that would install central services. Guest lectures were given on <span class="caps">GUMS</span> (Steve Timm), grid operations (Rob Quick), security   best practices (Anand Padmanabhan), and CE installation demonstration (Suchandra   Thapa).</p>         <p>Sites reported on their progress each day with problems encountered and next   steps. In the end, one site had functional Condor + <span class="caps">GUMS</span> + CE + Ganglia systems; three sites had working Condor systems plus additional   services, including working <span class="caps">GUMS</span> installations; one   installed an SE, and several had CE installs in various states of configuration   progress. The &ldquo;GOC team&rdquo; deployed a ReSS instance.</p>         <p>We devoted the final day to project planning and developing a program of work   for Grid Colombia based on progress made at the workshop. There are enthusiastic   plans to deploy twelve Grid Colombia sites by the end of the year using <span class="caps">OSG</span> software. Another workshop will occur in February/March,   at which <span class="caps">OSG</span> has been invited to provide a follow-up   component to the site-building modules given here and to offer guidance and   assistance in setting up their nascent grid.</p>         <p>Thank you to everyone who helped with the workshop&mdash;in particular, the guest   experts who stepped up with both intellectual content and personal effort.</p>         <p>More details: </p>         <p>- <a href="https://twiki.grid.iu.edu/bin/view/Education/GridColombiaWorkshop">Organizational Wiki</a></p>         <p>- <a href="https://twiki.grid.iu.edu/bin/view/ReleaseDocumentation/GridColombiaWorkshop2009">Technical Documentation</a> for practical exercises, starting points for future training</p>         <p>~ Rob Gardner and Jose Caballero</p>       </td>     </tr>   </tbody></table><p>___________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________</p><div class="writeboardtitle">   <p><b>From the Executive Director</b></p> </div><p>I much appreciate the focused attention and effort from the many people   involved in the recent and ongoing Tier-3 focus work, and I look forward to the   payback as new sites come on board. During the past month the US <span class="caps">ATLAS</span> Tier-3 coordinators, <span class="caps">OSG</span> Tier-3   liaison and site coordinators, and the Storage and Condor groups have had two   successful visits to the University of Wisconsin for &ldquo;hands-on standing up&rdquo; of a   typical Tier-3 site. This is being documented at the <a href="https://twiki.grid.iu.edu/bin/view/Tier3/WebHome">Tier 3 Web</a> and will become a pattern to   which we can add common instructions, best practices, and other information for   Tier-3 administrators&mdash;those who generally have only small amounts of time to   devote to this activity and who were not necessarily &ldquo;trained for this job.&rdquo;</p><p>This month&rsquo;s joint Identity Management Workshop between the <span class="caps">ESNET</span> DOEGrids and <span class="caps">OSG</span> groups   (Security, VOs and Blueprint) was energetic and useful. Thank you to all who   organized and attended this meeting. I look forward to the report and   recommendations for focus areas and decisions to be made, both short and long   term. An immediate response has been to couple the words &ldquo;Usability&rdquo; and   &ldquo;Security&rdquo; together when we talk&ndash;&mdash;with &ldquo;Usability&rdquo; coming first. The goal is for   this to concretely impact our actions as we move forward; several small   communities are now active for which this is currently a significant hurdle to   overcome.</p><p>The Open Science Grid magnetic clips are proving popular at SC09&mdash; &ldquo;The <span class="caps">OSG</span> clips your users and resources together.&rdquo; &mdash;where there has   been interest in <span class="caps">OSG</span> from potential users, our partners   in Europe, and our agency visitors.</p><p>~Ruth

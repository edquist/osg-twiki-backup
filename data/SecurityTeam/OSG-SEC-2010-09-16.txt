OSG Security Contacts

We want to inform you of an important security vulnerability affecting
RHEL/SL5 64-bit systems: 64-bit Compatibility Mode Stack Pointer Corruption.

The vulnerability is described at
https://bugzilla.redhat.com/show_bug.cgi?id=CVE-2010-3081                                 

The vulnerability allows any user on a vulnerable machine to elevate to
root.

Scientific Linux (SL) team at Fermilab has determined that: (SLF means SL Fermi
+distribution)

SL5 and SLF5 - x86_64 is vulnerable, the exploit works
SL5 and SLF5 - i386 is not vulnerable, the exploit fails.
SL4 and SLF4 - they are not vulnerable, the exploit fails.

An exploit is publicly available. However, there is no vendor patch
released yet. We will immediately inform you when it is available.

A GOC ticket exists at https://ticket.grid.iu.edu/goc/viewer?id=9241                      
With a valid DOEgrids certificate, all security contacts can view the
ticket and post questions/informations to the ticket.

A site can either shut down their systems until a patch is available or
continue operating while waiting for the patch. OSG Security team defers
to site security teams to make these decisions. If you choose to
continue operating, we advise you to monitor your systems more closely
until a patch is available.

WLCG management is aware of the issue and will accept essential and
related unscheduled downtime incurred while handling CVE-2010-3081.

If you like more specific advise regarding your site, please either
email osg-security-team@opensciencegrid.org, or update the GOC ticket
with your questions.

Regards
